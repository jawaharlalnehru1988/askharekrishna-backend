A **Sattvik kitchen** in both **Ayurveda** and **Krishna Consciousness (prasadam culture)** emphasizes foods that are:

* Pure, fresh, vegetarian
* Non-stimulating (no onion, garlic, coffee, tea)
* Balanced for digestion
* Cooked with devotion and offered before eating

Typical staples include **rice, dal, vegetables, ghee, milk products, grains, fruits, nuts, jaggery, mild spices**.

Cooking for Krishna (prasadam preparation) is not just culinary—it is a **theological discipline + consciousness training + purity system**. Below is a structured set of **core principles (minimum 25, expanded to ~40 for completeness)** aligned with **Gaudiya Vaishnava standards**.

# 🕉️ CORE PRINCIPLES FOR COOKING FOR KRISHNA

## 1. Foundational Consciousness Principles (Bhāva & Tattva)

1. **Cooking as Seva (Service)**
   Not “I am cooking”, but “I am serving Krishna”.

2. **Offering Mentality (Bhoga → Prasadam)**
   Food is meant for Krishna first, never for personal enjoyment.

3. **Remembrance of Krishna While Cooking**
   Chanting Hare Krishna or thinking of Krishna during preparation.

4. **Following Guru–Sadhu–Shastra**
   Cooking standards come from disciplic succession.

5. **Non-Enjoyer Principle**
   Cook is not the enjoyer—Krishna is the enjoyer.

6. **Gratitude Consciousness**
   Awareness that ingredients are Krishna’s mercy.

7. **Detachment from Taste**
   Cook for Krishna’s pleasure, not your tongue.

---

## 2. Purity & Cleanliness (Śauca)

8. **External Cleanliness (Body Hygiene)**
   Bathing or washing before cooking.

9. **Internal Cleanliness (Mental Purity)**
   Avoid anger, lust, agitation while cooking.

10. **Clean Kitchen Environment**
    Surfaces, utensils, stove—all must be clean.

11. **Dedicated Cooking Clothes**
    Preferably fresh, clean clothes.

12. **No Contamination Rules**
    No touching impure items during cooking.

13. **Separate Utensils for Offering**
    Bhoga utensils should be distinct.

---

## 3. Ingredient Principles (Āhāra Śuddhi)

14. **Strict Sattvik Ingredients Only**
    No onion, garlic, mushrooms, etc.

15. **No Meat, Fish, Eggs**
    Absolute vegetarian purity.

16. **Use of Fresh Ingredients**
    Avoid stale, processed, or leftover foods.

17. **Seasonal & Natural Foods Preferred**
    Align with Ayurvedic rhythms.

18. **Cow-based Products (Ghee, Milk)**
    Considered spiritually elevating.

19. **Avoid Tasting While Cooking**
    Food must remain untouched for Krishna.

---

## 4. Cooking Process Discipline

20. **Cook with Focus (No Distraction)**
    Avoid phone, gossip, or idle talk.

21. **Mantra Conscious Cooking**
    Chanting during preparation enhances purity.

22. **Mindful Handling of Ingredients**
    Respectful cutting, washing, cooking.

23. **Balanced Use of Spices**
    Not overly stimulating.

24. **Cooking with Love and Care**
    Bhakti transforms the food.

---

## 5. Offering (Bhoga Vidhi)

25. **Offer Before Eating**
    Never consume unoffered food.

26. **Use a Clean Plate for Offering**
    Reserved only for Krishna.

27. **Recitation of Offering Prayers**
    (e.g., prayers to Guru, Vaishnavas, Krishna)

28. **Silent & Respectful Offering Mood**
    No casual behavior during offering.

29. **Wait for Krishna to Accept**
    Allow time before removing plate.

---

## 6. Post-Offering Conduct (Prasadam Culture)

30. **Honor, Not Eat Prasadam**
    Mood of reverence.

31. **No Criticism of Prasadam**
    Never complain about taste.

32. **Distribute Prasadam Generously**
    Sharing is spiritual service.

33. **Respect Leftovers**
    Handle prasadam carefully.

---

## 7. Behavioral & Ethical Principles

34. **No Anger or Conflict in Kitchen**
    Mental state affects food subtlety.

35. **Avoid Ego (“I cooked well”)**
    Offer credit to Krishna.

36. **Cooperative Service Mood**
    Team cooking in harmony.

37. **Respect Senior Devotees’ Standards**
    Learn traditional methods.

---

## 8. Advanced Devotional Principles

38. **Cooking According to Time & Occasion**
    Festival vs daily offerings.

39. **Understanding Krishna’s Preferences**
    (e.g., butter, sweets, simple foods)

40. **Consistency in Standards**
    Maintain discipline daily, not occasionally.

# 🔥 What Most People Miss (Critical Insights)

* Cooking is **not culinary excellence → it is consciousness engineering**
* The **state of mind = hidden ingredient**
* Prasadam is **not symbolic → it is spiritually non-different from Krishna’s mercy**
* Violating small rules (like tasting) **breaks the offering integrity**
