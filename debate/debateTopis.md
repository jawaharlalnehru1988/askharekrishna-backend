# Debate Topics by Category

## Principles
- Non-sequiturs and logical fallacies
- Burden of proof and evidence standards
- Good faith argumentation
- Intellectual honesty and transparency
- Consistency and coherence in claims
- Appeal to authority vs. expertise
- Empirical vs. theoretical arguments

## Rules
- Turn-taking and speaking time limits
- Topic boundaries and scope
- Citation and source requirements
- Ad hominem attacks prohibition
- Semantic precision and definitions
- Cross-examination protocols
- Closing statements and rebuttals

## Tactics to Win Arguments
- Anticipate counterarguments
- Use strategic concessions
- Attack weakest opponent claims
- Control framing and definitions
- Ask clarifying questions
- Build credibility early
- Use data and case studies
- Identify and exploit logical gaps
- Practice rhetorical timing
- Summarize your wins before closing

## Tactics for Effective Conversation
- Active listening and acknowledgment
- Finding common ground
- Asking open-ended questions
- Avoiding defensive language
- Paraphrasing to confirm understanding
- Using "I" statements
- Respecting opposing viewpoints
- Managing emotional responses

## Saastric/Vedic Approaches to Debate

- Adherence to Shabda Pramana (authoritative texts as evidence)
- Siddhanta (established philosophical conclusions)
- Pramana and Pramaneya (means of knowledge and objects of knowledge)
- Anvaya-Vyatireka (positive and negative correlation analysis)
- Parampara (disciplic succession and lineage authority)
- Satya Pratijna (commitment to truth over winning)
- Samvada (respectful dialogue between equals)
- Tat Tvam Asi methodology (understanding through direct inquiry)
- Nyaya logic and syllogistic reasoning
- Yukti (rational inference and reasoning)
- Prataksha (direct perception and empirical observation)
- Aitihya (historical accounts and testimony)
- Shabdabodha (semantic analysis of language)
- Brahmasutra approach (systematic philosophical examination)
- Addressing Siddhanta vs. Pratiksha Siddhanta (primary vs. secondary positions)
- Guru-Sisya Parampara etiquette (teacher-student relationship respect)
- Pratijna-Hetu-Udaharana (proposition-reason-example framework)
- Vada vs. Jalpa vs. Vitanda (genuine debate vs. argument vs. destructive criticism)
- Recognition of different interpretive schools (Bhashya traditions)
- Emphasis on Moksha-Sadhana (spiritual liberation as ultimate context)
