A refined Vaiṣṇava personality is not built merely on external behavior—it is a synthesis of **śāstra-based conduct, inner purification, social sensitivity, and devotional alignment**. Below is a structured **taxonomy of Vaiṣṇava etiquette topics** you can systematically master to become a cultured, spiritually grounded gentleman.

---

# 🪔 1. Foundational Vaiṣṇava Character (Svarūpa Lakṣaṇa)

Core identity traits described in Bhagavad Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam

1. Humility (Amanitvam)
2. Freedom from pride
3. Non-violence (Ahimsa)
4. Tolerance (Titiksha)
5. Simplicity (Arjavam)
6. Truthfulness (Satya)
7. Compassion to all beings
8. Control of mind (Mano-nigraha)
9. Control of senses (Indriya-nigraha)
10. Cleanliness (Śaucam – internal & external)
11. Steadiness in dharma
12. Gratitude
13. Forgiveness (Kṣamā)
14. Detachment (Vairāgya)
15. Faith in guru and śāstra
16. Equipoise in happiness/distress
17. Respect for all living beings
18. Non-envy (Adveṣṭā sarva-bhūtānām)
19. Self-discipline
20. Inner satisfaction (Ātma-tṛpti)

---

# 🙏 2. Guru & Senior Vaiṣṇava Etiquette (Guru-sevā Ācāra)

1. Proper offering of obeisances (Daṇḍavat praṇām)
2. Speaking only when permitted
3. Not sitting higher than guru
4. Avoiding argument with guru
5. Serving without expectation
6. Receiving instructions submissively
7. Not interrupting spiritual discourse
8. Glorifying guru publicly and privately
9. Maintaining physical and mental cleanliness before meeting
10. Never seeing guru as ordinary human
11. Offering gifts with devotion
12. Observing silence in guru’s presence when needed
13. Protecting guru’s reputation
14. Following instructions strictly
15. Asking relevant and sincere questions

---

# 🧍‍♂️ 3. Personal Conduct & Daily Behavior (Ācāra)

1. Waking up during Brahma Muhurta
2. Daily chanting discipline (Japa etiquette)
3. Clean dressing (simple, sattvic)
4. Tilaka application etiquette
5. Regulated eating habits (Prasadam only)
6. Personal hygiene and purity
7. Speaking softly and truthfully
8. Avoiding gossip and criticism
9. Time discipline (punctuality)
10. Keeping surroundings clean
11. Walking and sitting modestly
12. Avoiding laziness
13. Maintaining spiritual focus during work
14. Eating only after offering to Krishna
15. Regulated sleep

---

# 🗣️ 4. Speech & Communication Etiquette (Vāk Tapas)

1. Speaking truth beneficially
2. Avoiding harsh words
3. Not speaking unnecessarily
4. Avoiding sarcasm and mockery
5. Speaking glorification of Krishna
6. Encouraging others spiritually
7. Avoiding argument for ego
8. Listening attentively
9. Avoiding idle talk (Prajalpa)
10. Speaking with humility
11. Avoiding offensive jokes
12. Using respectful titles (Prabhu, Mataji)
13. Avoiding backbiting
14. Controlled tone and volume
15. Speaking according to time, place, circumstance

---

# 🍽️ 5. Prasadam & Food Etiquette

1. Honoring only Krishna prasadam
2. Eating with gratitude
3. Not wasting food
4. Clean eating habits
5. Silence or Krishna-katha during meals
6. Avoiding overeating
7. Serving others first
8. Offering food properly before eating
9. Maintaining cleanliness in kitchen
10. Avoiding criticizing prasadam
11. Accepting whatever is given
12. Eating at regulated times

---

# 🛕 6. Temple Etiquette (Mandir Maryāda)

1. Entering with clean body and mind
2. Offering obeisances immediately
3. Not turning back to Deities
4. Maintaining silence
5. Proper dress code
6. Not stretching legs toward altar
7. Circumambulation etiquette
8. Respecting temple schedule
9. Not touching Deities without qualification
10. Participating in kirtan attentively
11. Not using mobile inside temple
12. Avoiding casual behavior
13. Respecting sacred items
14. Sitting properly during class
15. Leaving quietly

# 🤝 7. Vaiṣṇava Association Etiquette (Sādhu-saṅga)

1. Offering respects to all devotees
2. Not judging others’ advancement
3. Avoiding offenses (Vaiṣṇava aparādha)
4. Serving devotees sincerely
5. Sharing prasadam
6. Encouraging others in bhakti
7. Avoiding competition mentality
8. Honoring even junior devotees
9. Maintaining unity
10. Avoiding politics
11. Forgiving faults
12. Appreciating good qualities
13. Avoiding criticism
14. Cooperating in service
15. Being approachable and kind

# ⚠️ 8. Avoiding Offenses (Aparādha Awareness)

Based on teachings emphasized by A. C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

1. 10 Nāma-aparādhas (Holy Name offenses)
2. Vaiṣṇava aparādha (offending devotees)
3. Seva aparādha (temple/service offenses)
4. Guru aparādha
5. Śāstra nindā (criticizing scriptures)
6. Considering demigods equal to Krishna
7. Maintaining material attachments
8. Inattention in chanting
9. Pride in devotional service
10. Disrespect to prasadam
